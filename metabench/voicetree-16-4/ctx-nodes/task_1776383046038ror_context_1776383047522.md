## What M5 is (from writeup-v2.md §Scoring decomposition)
> **M5 (decomposition effectiveness):** per-run `AUC_model / AUC_ceiling`; mean across a cell gives per-model per-cell score.
Where:
- `AUC_model` = trapezoidal area under the per-turn score trajectory `score_t = 100 − gap_pct_t` across the run's exec turns
- `AUC_ceiling` = area under the model's own cell ceiling held flat across the same turn count. The cell ceiling is the max *final* score observed for that `(model, class, difficulty)` cell in the overnight pilot (3 seeds per cell = 3 candidates for the max).
Per-row M5 ∈ roughly (0, 1+]. >1 is possible if a row beats the cell ceiling; don't clip. Report both raw and the percentage of rows where M5 ≥ 1. Capability-controlled by design: a model that has low ceiling in a cell but gets close to its own ceiling fast earns high M5; a high-capability model that lazily stops at t=1 earns low M5 relative to its own ceiling.
