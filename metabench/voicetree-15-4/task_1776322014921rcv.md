# Problem class — coupled job-shop, NL framing

Classic F2||Cmax (two-machine flowshop minimum makespan). Each job passes through Department A then Department B. One job at a time per department. Makespan = total time to finish all jobs.

- **Johnson's rule gives the optimal ordering in O(n log n)** — so we have cheap gold.
- **Greedy (SPT on A, SPT on B, random, etc.) is suboptimal** — the coupling creates a trap.
- **Tunable difficulty** — scale n_jobs (8 is easy, 15 is meaty, 25 pushes limits).

## Framing in natural language (no JSON, no lists of numbers in structured form)

Example (tune for the spike):
> "You run a small manufacturing shop. You have 12 orders to complete today. Every order has to go through two stations: first prep (Department A), then finishing (Department B). Department A handles one order at a time; same for B. You can choose the order you process them in, and that order is the same across both departments. You want to finish all 12 orders as quickly as possible (minimize total completion time — the time between starting order 1 in Department A and finishing the last order in Department B).
>
> Here are today's orders with their times in minutes:
> - Order #1: 7 min prep, 4 min finish
> - Order #2: 3 min prep, 11 min finish
> ... [12 orders total]
>
> A clerk already drafted an order sequence in order of arrival: #1, #2, #3, ..., #12. With that baseline sequence the shop would finish at **BASELINE_MAKESPAN = 147 minutes**.
>
> Try to find a sequence that finishes faster."

The expected output contract (per locked protocol):
- Turn 1: `ATOMIC_PREDICTION`, `DECLARED_GAP`, `DECISION`, `NEXT_SUB`
- Turn N≥2: `SUB_{n}` (work), `BEST_GUESS: <ordered list like 2,5,1,7,...>`, `P_CORRECT`, `DECISION`, `NEXT_SUB`
- Answer = the sequence. Score against Johnson's-rule optimal makespan. `BEST_GUESS` is a permutation — we compute its makespan programmatically.

# Implementation checklist

1. **Instance generator** (`jobshop_instance.py`): n=12 jobs, processing times uniform in [1,20], seeded. Compute:
   - Johnson's-rule optimal sequence + optimal makespan (gold)
   - Arrival-order baseline makespan (shipped in problem)
2. **Problem rendering** (`render_nl.py`): turn the instance into flowing natural language — no tables, no bullet lists of numbers in markdown tables. Each order one short sentence. Include baseline makespan explicitly.
3. **Single canonical prompt** (`prompt.py`): one system prompt that frames the economic incentive (stop-when-marginal-gain-<-marginal-cost), tells the agent about the 30 min / 10 min / 5 min budgets, and states the output contract. No arm variants.
4. **Protocol harness**: import from `hch/metagame/protocol.py`. Keep turn mechanics identical.
5. **Parsing**: `BEST_GUESS` is a permutation. Robust regex — tolerate `[2, 5, 1, ...]`, `2,5,1,...`, `2 → 5 → 1 → ...`, etc. Validate it's a permutation of the job numbers; if not, treat as no-update.
6. **Scoring** (`analyze.py`): `gap_pct = 100 * (makespan(guess) - gold_makespan) / gold_makespan`, `brier = (declared_gap - gap_pct)²`, `$score = 1.0 * max(0, 100 - gap_pct) - 0.01 * total_wall_s`. Protocol flags: turn1_died, parse_fail, subtask_killed_count, revised_best_guess_downward.
7. **Runner** (`run_spike.py`): 3 models × 1 arm × 3 seeds (can bump to 5 if time permits). Write to `results/jobshop_<timestamp>.jsonl`.
