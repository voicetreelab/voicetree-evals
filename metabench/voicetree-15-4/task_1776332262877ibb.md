# Key observations from conversation context (synthesize these)

- **Every spike has one-shot behavior.** TSP-25, Meg v1 flowshop, Meg v2 5x6, Mei 3x4, Mei 6x7, Nia Steiner×coloring N=8 and N=12 — ALL show: 1 plan turn + 1 exec turn → stop. No multi-turn iteration has ever fired on Gemini 3.1 Pro in these experiments.
- **A1 miscalibration is the one clean signal we've measured.** Mei's 6x7 jobshop: declared 100/135/120% gap, achieved 1.75/3.03/8.49%. `atomic_p_correct=0` on all three.
- **Composite coupling DOES produce joint reasoning.** Nia N=8: Gemini explicitly identified the interference triangle and optimized jointly. The composite pattern is NOT pattern-matched to a recipe at the coupling level, even when components are named ("Steiner tree").
- **300s plan-turn death with rich A1 contract is consistent** across Mei jobshop + Nia Steiner×coloring. Likely thinking-mode trying to solve the whole problem during plan. Nia stripped the contract to unblock; this sacrificed A1 signal.
- **Scaling alone doesn't force multi-turn.** N=12 Steiner×coloring: still one-shot, and 2/3 seeds failed to even improve baseline.
- **SCIP-Jack did not work for our composite** (problem mismatch); brute-force exact solver does not scale past N≈8.
